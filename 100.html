The more things change, the more they stay the same.


Our models are based on roughly one year of tweets from @realDonaldTrump. In his first 100-days in office, some things have changed in his Twitter use -- most significantly trading in his old Android for a new iPhone as his Tweet-vehicle of choice.

But even taking on the highest office in the land wasn't enough to really change Mr. Trump's Twitter habits. His language, timing and tactics remains remarkably similar to before his inauguration and consistent in many ways with David Robinson's great analysis from last year.

Sleep is for losers. And probably staffers.

One of the biggest differences between a tweet directly from Mr. Trump versus those of his staffers is time. This held true in his first 100-days. You can see the difference in likely tweets from Mr. Trump since inauguration -- peaking roughly 6am-10am (GMT-4) -- versus those of his staffers during the more normal business hours of 11am-7pm. About this site
